A full week of Instagram content in 30 minutes

No blank screen. No agency. Just Claude, a one-time brand brief, and a repeatable Sunday-night system.

By Kate · Savage Social, Arizona
Swipe →
Before you start

Have three things written down

  • What your business does — in one plain sentence.
  • Who your ideal customer is — be specific.
  • Three words for your brand voice.

Plus one free scheduler (Meta Business Suite works). No video gear, no design skills needed.

1

Start a Claude Project + write your brand brief

Create a Project for your business. It stores your context permanently, so Claude remembers who you are every time — no re-explaining.

Drop your business, customer, voice, and #1 client result into the Project instructions.

2

Define your three content pillars

  • Teach — tips and how-tos that solve one real problem.
  • Show — behind-the-scenes and real client results.
  • Sell — what you offer and the after-state it creates.

Three categories is all you need. Add them to your Project.

3

Prompt for your 7-post calendar

Ask Claude for a week: 2 Teach, 2 Show, 2 Sell, 1 engagement post.

"Using my brand brief and pillars, create a 7-post Instagram calendar. For each: the hook, the main point in 2–3 sentences, and a CTA."
4

Turn the calendar into full captions

Convert each outline into a publishable caption, one at a time.

"Write a full caption for Post 3 [outline]. My brand voice. Under 200 words. First line is the hook. End with [your CTA]."

Most drafts come back 80–90% usable on the first pass.

5

Edit for your voice — this is where you win

  • Read the hook out loud. Won't say it? Rewrite it.
  • Add one detail only you'd know — a name, a real number.
  • Make the CTA sound like you, not a template.

65% of people now scroll past obvious AI. Two minutes per post fixes it.

6

Schedule it and walk away

Paste all seven into Meta Business Suite, Later, or Buffer. Add your images, set the times, close the app.

Save the brief in your Project and next Sunday takes 20 minutes, not 30.
