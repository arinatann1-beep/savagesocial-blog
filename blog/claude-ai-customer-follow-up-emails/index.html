Write a week of customer follow-up emails with Claude in 20 minutes

By Savage Social  ·  820 words  ·  5 min read

Most business owners have a lead sitting in their inbox right now who went quiet after the first conversation. Not because they lost interest. Because following up is awkward, time-consuming, and it's never obvious what to say without sounding pushy.

Claude can write your next five follow-up emails in about 20 minutes. All you do is review them and hit send.

Here's why most follow-up emails fail: they're all pressure and no value, or so generic they could have been sent to anyone. Claude fixes both problems when you give it the right input. The sequence below is built around what actually moves people to respond — not what feels polite in the moment.

Get Claude set up (the free plan is enough to start)

Go to claude.ai and create a free account. The free plan handles email drafting without issue. No integrations, no plugins, no setup — you're working directly in the chat interface.

Write your Business Context Brief

This is the most important step. Before you write a single email, give Claude everything it needs to write in your voice. Paste this at the top of every Claude session:

You are a copywriter for [Your Business Name]. We are a [type of business] based in [location] serving [ideal client type]. Our tone is [warm / professional / direct]. Our clients come to us because they want [main result]. We never [anything you don't say or do]. Write as one human speaking to one other human. Never use corporate filler phrases.

Fill this in with real specifics. The more concrete your input, the less editing you'll do on the output.

Map your five-email sequence before you open Claude

You need five emails, each with a distinct purpose:

  • Day 2: Light check-in, no pressure
  • Day 5: Value add — a tip, result, or relevant resource
  • Day 10: Social proof — a client win relevant to their situation
  • Day 18: Gentle nudge — "still thinking about this?"
  • Day 30: Graceful exit — leave the door open, no hard feelings

Write these five purposes down before you start. You're directing Claude, not waiting for it to decide.

Use this prompt for each email

After pasting your Business Context Brief, run this once per email:

Write Email [number] of a five-part follow-up sequence for a prospect named [First Name] who I spoke with on [date]. They were interested in [service] but haven't responded. Purpose of this email: [paste one purpose from step 3]. What I remember about their situation: [1-2 sentences]. Keep under 150 words. Include a subject line. Friendly but professional.

Run this five times. With a free account, five drafts take roughly 15 to 18 minutes total.

Edit for your voice (two minutes per email, not more)

Read each draft out loud. If anything sounds stiff or unlike you, change only that sentence. Look for generic sign-offs, press-release phrases, and one place per email where you can add a detail only you would know. This is what separates emails that get replies from ones that get archived.

Schedule your sends

Load the five emails into your email client or CRM. Set send dates: Day 2, 5, 10, 18, 30. Gmail's native scheduling tool handles this natively. So does HubSpot, Mailchimp, and most CRMs.

You now have a complete five-touchpoint follow-up system for this lead. Run this for every new conversation and your pipeline has consistent follow-through that requires nothing from you after the initial 20-minute session. The whole process takes 20 minutes the first time. By the third or fourth lead you run it for, you're under 15.

This is the same system that gets service businesses consistent callbacks on warm leads. The difference between closing 1 in 10 leads and 3 in 10 is usually not price or pitch quality. It's follow-through, done without being annoying.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use the free version of Claude to write customer follow-up emails?

Yes. Claude's free plan at claude.ai handles email drafting without issue. Paid plans provide faster responses and longer outputs, but the core workflow in this guide runs on the free tier.

How do I make Claude-written emails sound like me?

Start every Claude session with a Business Context Brief describing your business, clients, tone, and phrases you never use. Then read each draft out loud and swap any stiff phrases for words you'd actually say. Two minutes of editing per email is enough.

How many follow-up emails should I send before moving on from a lead?

Five, spread across 30 days. HubSpot research consistently shows most service business conversions happen between the third and fifth touchpoint. One or two follow-ups is leaving money on the table.
