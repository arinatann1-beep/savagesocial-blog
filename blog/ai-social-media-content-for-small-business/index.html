Use Claude to create a week of Instagram content before your next coffee gets cold

By Savage Social  ·  920 words  ·  5 min read

Thirty minutes from now, you can have a full week of Instagram content written, reviewed, and ready to schedule. No staring at a blank screen. No paying someone for a first draft you will spend an hour rewriting. Just Claude, a short brand brief you write once, and a repeatable workflow you can run every Sunday evening.

This is the exact process I walk clients through when we are setting up their content systems. You can run the full workflow right now using Claude's free plan or paid plan, with no design tools and no copywriting experience required.

Here is the complete play.

What you need before you start

Before opening Claude, have three things written down: what your business does, who your ideal customer is (be specific), and three words that describe your brand voice. You also need a free scheduling tool — Meta Business Suite works for Instagram and Facebook and costs nothing. That is all. No video equipment, no graphic design, no agency.

Start a Claude Project and write your brand brief

In Claude, click "Projects" in the left sidebar and create a new project for your business. Projects store your brand context permanently so Claude remembers who you are every time you open it, without you re-explaining anything.

In the Project instructions, paste this template and fill it in:

My business is [type of business]. I serve [describe your specific customer, e.g., "women 35-55 who own service businesses in Arizona"]. My brand voice is [e.g., warm, direct, confident]. My main service is [X]. The number one result clients get from working with me is [specific outcome]. My content never uses jargon. I write in first person and always speak to one person, not a crowd.

The more specific you are here, the more every single post Claude writes will sound like you wrote it yourself.

Define your three content pillars

Before asking Claude for posts, give it the framework for what you post about. Three categories is all you need. A simple example for a service-business owner:

  • Teach: Tips, how-tos, and data that help your ideal customer solve a specific problem
  • Show: Behind-the-scenes, real client results, day-in-the-life content
  • Sell: What you offer, who it is for, and what a client's life looks like after working with you

Add these to your Project instructions alongside your brand brief.

Prompt Claude for your 7-post content calendar

Now you are ready. Paste this prompt directly into your Claude Project:

Using my brand brief and my three content pillars, create a 7-post Instagram content calendar for this week. Include: 2 Teach posts that answer a specific question my audience has, 2 Show posts (one client result and one behind-the-scenes), 2 Sell posts that highlight what my service does for a client, and 1 engagement post that asks my audience a direct question. For each post, give me: the hook (opening line only), the main point (2-3 sentences), and a suggested call to action.

Claude returns a structured calendar with all seven post outlines. Review it, swap a topic if one does not fit your week, and move to the next step.

Turn the calendar into full captions

Now convert each outline into a publishable caption. Run this prompt for each post, one at a time:

Write a full Instagram caption for Post 3 [paste the outline here]. Use my brand voice. Keep it under 200 words. The first line is the hook. End with [your specific CTA, e.g., "comment X for the guide" or "book a free consult at the link in bio"].

Run through all seven posts. Most drafts come back 80-90% usable on the first pass.

Edit for your voice, because this is where you win

HubSpot's 2026 State of Marketing found 65% of consumers are getting better at identifying AI content and scrolling past it. The fix takes under 2 minutes per post.

For each caption, do three things:

  • Read the opening line out loud. If you would not actually say those words, rewrite it in your own.
  • Add one specific detail only you would know: a client's first name, a real number from your business, a specific situation that happened last week.
  • Check the call to action. Does it sound like you talking, or like a fill-in-the-blank template?

Small edits, significant difference in how the post performs.

Schedule and walk away

Paste all seven captions into Meta Business Suite, Later, or Buffer. Add your images or video clips. Set the posting times and close the app. You now have a full week of content in the queue with time to spare.

One thing that makes this faster every week: save your brand brief and your three pillars inside your Claude Project permanently. Next Sunday, open the same project, run the same calendar prompt, and the whole process takes 20 minutes instead of 30. After a few rounds, you will know exactly which post formats are driving your most inquiries and which to replace.

This workflow is exactly how consistently posting small-business owners are keeping their feed active without burning hours every week on content. The difference between the ones showing up every day and the ones posting once a month is not motivation. It is a system.
